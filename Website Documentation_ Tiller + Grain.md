## 1. Introduction

This document presents a detailed analysis and documentation of the content and structure of the **Tiller + Grain** website [1], a café in Dublin 2, focusing on seasonal salads, sandwiches, and bakery items. The documentation was prepared by navigating page by page, with the aim of mapping the information architecture and the textual content available to the public.

## 3. Detailed Content Analysis

### 3.1. Philosophy and Suppliers (About Our Food)

Tiller + Grain's core philosophy is:

> "Take the best of fresh, seasonal Irish produce, treat it well and treat it simply. We make everything fresh and daily with taste and nutrition being at the core of our food. We are invested in our local economy and our mission is to do justice to the amazing farmers and local producers we have on this island." [2]

The website highlights the commitment to Irish suppliers and sustainable farming methods:

| Category | Cited Suppliers |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Meat** | JJ Young and Sons |
| **Fish** | Sustainable Seafood Ireland |
| **Artisan Produce** | Lilliput Stores |
| **Vegetables/Salads** | Airfield Farm, Sean Hussey & Sons |

### 3.2. Menu and Ordering (Menus & Ordering)

The ordering section details service options and the seasonal menu.

#### 3.2.1. Daily Menu (Salads, Meats, and Fish)

The menu is seasonal and focused on salads and main courses with clearly indicated allergens.

| Dish Type | Item Examples (Seasonal) | Allergens (Examples) |

#### 3.2.2. Tiller + Trip Boxes (Staycation Box)

The boxes are described as "ready-to-eat things" for trips or stays, with prices based on the number of people:

- **€80** for 2 people
- **€160** for 4 people
- **€240** for 6 people

48 hours' notice is required for ordering.
